II The Studio

Aira implies the beginning. We plan weddings across India — quietly, with attention to the smallest of details and the warmth of a family kitchen.

Led by Sarang, our creative head, the Aira team has spent the last decade making South-Indian weddings feel like the editorials they deserve to be. Mehendis, haldis, sangeets, ceremonies, receptions — and the quieter celebrations in between.

V Work

A carousel
of our work.

Twenty-one weddings, twenty-one different days — slide through a few of our favourites.

No. 01 · Kerala, 2024

An enchanting French chateau.

Customised European-villa decor, transposed into Kerala backwaters. Pastel blooms, candle-lit walkways, a lawn ceremony at sunset.

No. 02 · Alleppey beach

The wedding jenga.

An intimate beach-front celebration on the Alleppey shore. Bare feet, a wooden mandap, and a sunset that played the role of art director.

No. 03 · Rose-petal mandap

A pathway to heaven.

A petal-strewn aisle the bride still talks about. Two thousand roses, three weeks of conditioning, one perfect afternoon.

No. 04 · Poolside · Marigold

The yellow-tassel mehendi.

Marigold drapes overhead, brass urlis at every corner, henna artists at twelve stations. Three hours of drying, four hours of stories.

No. 05 · Pastel drapes · Chandeliers

Reception, the English way.

Crystal chandeliers, ivory drapes, a string quartet. A reception that read like the second act of a film set in Cornwall.

No. 06 · Bengaluru · Mela-themed

Timeless fun over the mandalas.

A mela-themed sangeet — kulhads of chai, painted rangoli, a dhol that didn't stop until 2 a.m.

No. 07 · White & Yellow florals

The chrysanthemum haldi.

White-and-yellow florals against a sun-bleached courtyard. Turmeric, song, and a grandmother's hand on every cheek.

No. 08 · Modern evening

The perfect contemporary.

Brushed-metal table settings, candle-lit bar, a typographic dance floor. A wedding that could have been on the cover of a design magazine.

No. 09 · Multi-family celebration

A blissful amalgamation of tradition.

Two cultures, one altar — North and South in conversation through the entire weekend.

No. 10 · Alleppey shores

A peachy tide in Kerala.

Peach satin, hand-painted invitations, an open-sky reception. The kind of day that makes every photographer slow their shutter.

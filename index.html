II The Studio

Aira implies the beginning. We plan weddings across South India — quietly, with attention to the smallest of details and the warmth of a family kitchen.

Led by Sarang, our creative head, the Aira team has spent the last decade making South-Indian weddings feel like the editorials they deserve to be. Mehendis, haldis, sangeets, ceremonies, receptions — and the quieter celebrations in between.

From the smallest of details to the largest of arrangements, our attention is the same. Each wedding is planned, designed, and run as if it were the only one we'll ever do.

III Work

Twenty-one weddings,
twenty-one different days.

01

An enchanting French chateau.

Customised European-villa decor, transposed into Kerala backwaters. Pastel blooms, candle-lit walkways, a lawn ceremony at sunset.

02

The wedding jenga.

An intimate beach-front celebration on the Alleppey shore. Bare feet, a wooden mandap, and a sunset that played the role of art director.

03

A pathway to heaven.

A petal-strewn aisle that the bride still talks about. Two thousand roses, three weeks of conditioning, one perfect afternoon.

04

The yellow-tassel mehendi.

Marigold drapes overhead, brass urlis at every corner, henna artists at twelve stations. Three hours of drying, four hours of stories.

05

Reception, the English way.

Crystal chandeliers, ivory drapes, a string quartet. A reception that read like the second act of a film set in Cornwall.

06

Timeless fun over the mandalas.

A mela-themed sangeet — kulhads of chai, painted rangoli, a dhol that didn't stop until 2 a.m.

07

The chrysanthemum haldi.

White-and-yellow florals against a sun-bleached courtyard. Turmeric, song, and a grandmother's hand on every cheek.

08

The perfect contemporary.

Brushed-metal table settings, candle-lit bar, a typographic dance floor. A wedding that could have been on the cover of a design magazine.

09

A blissful amalgamation
of tradition.

Two cultures, one altar — North and South in conversation through the entire weekend.

10

A peachy tide in Kerala.

Peach satin, hand-painted invitations, an open-sky reception. The kind of day that makes every photographer slow their shutter.

11

Victorian dreams
come true.

Velvet ottomans, brass candelabras, a string of pearls hanging from the ceiling. Old-world cinema brought to a Bengaluru ballroom.

12

The grandeur begins here.

French-chateau decor at noon — open lawns, parasols, glass urns of garden roses. A wedding that began the day, instead of ending it.

13

Divine & exotic,
a euphoric combination.

A floral installation that took six florists three days. Forty thousand stems, mostly local, mostly seasonal, all arranged by hand.

14

Dance-away kinda evening.

Neon, leather, a live band. The bride's father did "Born to Run" — fully committed.

15

Red & white magic.

A velvet ceiling and a chandelier of tuberose. The mandap was small enough to feel intimate, theatrical enough to feel like opera.

16

A paradise beginning.

The Ganapathi installation under the tamarind tree — the kind of detail that makes the elders smile and the photographers stop walking.

17

Cocktail evening
at The Leela.

An American-English hybrid — gin bar at one end, kebab counter at the other, a jazz trio in the middle. Everyone stayed past midnight.

18

Gala "full" haldi.

A haldi that turned into a colour-throw, a brunch, a swim, and a DJ set in the same afternoon. We tracked thirty kilos of turmeric.

19

Those little details.

Embroidered name tags. Hand-painted menus. Coasters made of pressed leaves. The kind of detail nobody asks for but everyone notices.

20

A stage of grace.

Traditional in form, contemporary in proportion. The kind of mandap that holds a thousand guests' eyes and a single couple's vows.

21

Lounges like these.

A low-seated, cushion-strewn mehendi lounge under a pergola. Drinks at hand, henna in progress, time deliberately slowing down.
